About the Map

american_dreams.temp is a data visualization project completed as the final project for 2018 Spring Javascript for Planners and Designers course at PennDesign.

H1B Visa is a form of U.S. work authorization that is petitioned by the employer to request hiring of high-skilled foreign labor. Often entangled with the discussions of American job loss, H1B has received considerable heat, especially given that the fast growth of tech industries have heavily relied upon many who have been allowed to work under this visa status.

Data displayed on here simply presents the result of petition requests, the process that precedes the USCIS Random Selection stage ("the lottery"). Through the lottery, total of 65,000 regular quota and 20,000 US Maste's quota are met. In other words, only those that are displayed as "certified" on this map will be given the chance to enter the lottery proceedings.

This project was motivated to inspire insights that go beyond the arduous undertaking that promises (at the most) six years of work authorization -- the modern story of american dreams. The same story of perseverance on this land, but this one belongs to non-immigrants, only temporarily.

Use this map to explore the pockets of this country they find communities and contribute to local economies. Where is the shortage of quality high-skilled labor most pronounced?

***************
TOGGLE filters on the sidebar to subset data.
ZOOM in and out to switch between county-level and state-level aggregations.
HOVER over the city points to find out from where the most number of H1B visa petitions originate -- nationally and locally.
CLICK each geography to display additional information about the area on the sidebar.
***************

note: Currently, this interactive map only displays the H1B petitions that were released for the 2017Q2 period (Jan - Mar 2017). This is the most recent dataset that is available for Q2s, which is when the volume of petition filings peak annually. This best highlights interregional variations. Going forward, this map will be improved to include a time slider that will allow users to visualize over time.
